Summary:

In this world there seem to be two camps of Geeks. There are the "Matrix" geeks who desire to live in a world of mental uplinks, deck boards, and to hear whispers to their ghost while in a new shell. Then there are the "Forest geeks" who desire nothing more than a multihome OC-48 cabin in the woods where people leave them alone.

I am one of the latter. I make no excuses, this project is a direct outlet from my quirky desires.

The goal is simple. To have the most rugged and indestructible portable lan-party and internet access vehichle that is possible. That is which I desire. I want to be able to drive up to Northern Ontario or through the Great Dismal swamp and still check my E-mail, read slashdot, and write webpages. I want to be able to read Sluggy Freelance when there is no one else around me for 150 miles.

Deep forest computing.

The Brand:

The choice of vehichle was actually simple. Some of you may or may not be familiar with the legacy of the Land Rover. I am not talking about the fancy SUVs that your prissy executive bosses buy and drive back and forth to work.

The Land Rover was Britan's equivalent to the American Jeep. It was technically superior in many ways, from a rust-proof aircraft aluminum skin, to a inch and a half steel firewall.

Since World War II the rover company took pride in building a practically indestructible vehichle, taking aim for the Farming and heavy-usage market.

The older series rover's where vehichles that could survive the poutback desert, the frozen north, the swamps of south america, and anything else that was remotely driveable. It has been made into tractors, ambulances, dropped out of planes, turned into desert survival vehichles. But I am getting off track. For an introduction, check out the intro from the Landrover FAQ more rover history.

Let's just say that there are very few vehichles in this world that can be field dressed with a flat blade screwdriver and a crescent wrench. This is one, thus this is the one.

The Vehicle:

The alpha release.

A good friend of mine pointed me to a dealership in my area that recently started to import old series rovers from England.

Skytop Rover, Inc. is currently importing the vehichles that the British military are retiring. After kicking the tires and going for a test ride or two, I selected my final contender.

The picture on the right is the rover I bought, right on the lot at Skytop. It was retired from the BerkShire sharpshooters regiment. This was an officer's vehichle. Unlike most of the other vehichles, it was not a direct Military manufacturer. Instead of being mil-spec 24-volt, this is a civilian chassis 12-volt that had been fitted.

All of the vehichles in this batch were convoy rigged radio vehichles. Many of them were fixed with oversized alternators with idle throttles to support radio broadcast. This vehichle, with radio fittings and such, though, didn't have the goant alternator.
